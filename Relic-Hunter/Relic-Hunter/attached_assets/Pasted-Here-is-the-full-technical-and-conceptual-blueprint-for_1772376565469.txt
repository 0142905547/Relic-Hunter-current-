Here is the full technical and conceptual blueprint for Relic Hunter: The Daily Dungeon.1. The Narrative EngineThe app doesn't just list tasks; it translates your life into a fantasy narrative.The Hero: Your customizable avatar. Their appearance changes based on your most-leveled stats (e.g., high "Intelligence" gives them a Mage's robe; high "Strength" gives them Plate Armor).The World Map: A procedurally generated map that resets every 24 hours. Your "Home Base" is 8:00 AM, and the "Final Boss" is your deadline or evening wind-down.2. Task Architecture (The "Bestiary")In this app, tasks have "Weight" and "Type," which dictates how they appear in the dungeon:Critters (Quick Wins): Tasks taking <10 mins (e.g., making the bed). These appear as weak goblins. They provide fast XP but low Gold.Elites (Deep Work): Tasks taking 1–2 hours. These are mini-bosses. They require "Stamina" (a timer) to defeat.World Bosses (Major Goals): Large projects. These have a Progress Bar (HP) that might take several days of "attacks" to deplete.Environmental Hazards (Habits): Things like "Drink Water" or "Stretch." If you ignore these, your screen gets "poisoned" (blurry or darker) until you complete them.3. The "Stamina & Mana" SystemTo prevent the app from being a mindless checklist, it uses two resource bars:Stamina ($S$): Depletes as you check off physical or high-effort tasks.Recovery: Log a "Rest Task" (nap, meal, meditation).Mana ($M$): Used for creative or "Focus" tasks.Recovery: Sleep tracking or leisure time.$$Current Performance = \frac{S + M}{2}$$If your resources are too low, the app suggests "Side Quests" (easier tasks) instead of "Main Quests."4. The Economy & "The Item Shop"Gold earned from tasks isn't just for digital vanity. It bridges the gap between the app and the real world.Digital Rewards: New skins, pets, or "Scrolls of Teleportation" (which allow you to skip a task guilt-free once a week).Real-World Rewards: You can create "Custom Loot."Example: "Buy 1 hour of Video Games" costs 500 Gold.Example: "Order Pizza" costs 2,000 Gold.This forces the user to earn their leisure time through productivity.5. Technical Features & UIThe "Battle" Interface: When you start a task, the app enters a Focus Mode. A pixel-art animation of your character fighting a monster plays. A Pomodoro timer acts as the "Attack Speed."The Skill Tree: You don't just "Level Up." You choose a path:The Titan (Health/Fitness focus)The Sage (Learning/Career focus)The Artisan (Creativity/Hobby focus)The Graveyard: At the end of the month, see a list of "Defeated Bosses" (completed projects) to give a sense of long-term scale.6. The "Anti-Guilt" Mechanic: The TavernMost productivity apps make you feel like a failure if you miss a day. In Relic Hunter, if you have a bad day, you go to The Tavern.You check in as "Wounded."The app pauses all streaks and high-level quests.It offers "Recovery Quests" like "Drink a glass of water" or "Stand up for 1 minute" to help you get back on your feet without the shame of a "broken streak."
